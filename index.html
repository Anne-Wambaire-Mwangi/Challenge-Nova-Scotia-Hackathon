The Problem

A Healthcare System Under Strain

Problem Statement

Nova Scotia faces a healthcare accessibility crisis. Emergency rooms are overwhelmed with non-urgent visits, driving average wait times to 3.5 hours — and over 10 hours in congested areas. The 811 tele-nurse service, designed as a first line of guidance, reports callback delays exceeding 4 hours. Meanwhile, over 87,000 Nova Scotians (8.2% of the population) remain on the waitlist for a family doctor, leaving them without a primary care pathway entirely.

The result: residents are uncertain where to go, when to go, and how urgently they need care. Non-urgent patients flood emergency departments. People who need urgent attention wait alongside those who don't. And the system strains further under the load.

NovaCare was designed to address this gap — an AI-assisted self-screening tool that helps residents assess their symptoms, understand the urgency of their condition, and get routed to the right level of care before they walk into an ER or dial 811.

The Solution

Four Triage Pathways

NovaCare guides users through symptom assessment and routes them to one of four care pathways, with red-flag detection for emergencies. The AI handles pattern recognition and follow-up questions — the human makes the final decision.

Emergency

Immediate ER / 911

Red-flag symptoms like severe chest pain, difficulty breathing, or loss of consciousness trigger an immediate emergency screen with a direct 911 call link. No further screening — user safety first.

Urgent

Contact 811 or Visit ER

Conditions requiring professional assessment but not immediately life-threatening. Users get direct links to 811 and nearby clinics with wait time information.

Moderate

Clinic or Virtual Consult

Conditions that benefit from professional consultation within 24–48 hours. Self-care tips provided alongside clinic and pharmacy locators.

Self-Care

Home Remedies & OTC

Symptoms manageable at home with rest, hydration, and over-the-counter medication. Clear escalation guidance if the condition changes.

Human–AI Collaboration, Not Replacement

NovaCare is built on the principle that AI should augment human judgment — never replace it. The tool assists with symptom pattern recognition, triage logic, and care routing, but every recommendation includes clear disclaimers, escalation pathways, and encouragement to seek professional advice. Health officials remain the final authority.

Key App Features
🏥

Hospital & Clinic Locator

Find nearby hospitals with estimated ER wait times and emergency service availability. Geolocation-based search with map view and clickable phone numbers.

💊

Pharmacy Locator

Discover nearby pharmacies including primary care clinics. Displays hours, distance, and contact info with direction support via Google Maps.

🤖

AI Symptom Assessment

Conversational AI asks contextual follow-up questions based on reported symptoms, demographics, and medical history — capped at 5–7 questions to avoid fatigue.

🚨

Red-Flag Detection

Critical symptoms (chest pain, severe bleeding, loss of consciousness) immediately bypass screening and display emergency guidance with a direct 911 dial link.

Prioritization

MoSCoW Requirements

Requirements scoped and prioritized to drive the initial proof of concept, with clear boundaries on what's in and out for V1.

Must Have

  • Digital screening questionnaire
  • Automated triage results
  • Secure data storage & encryption
  • Clear action steps post-screening
  • Mobile-friendly interface
  • PHIA-compliant data handling
  • Basic audit logging

Should Have

  • Clinician summary dashboard
  • Error handling & fallback
  • Exportable screening summaries
  • Admin interface for questions

Could Have

  • Multi-language support
  • Dark mode interface
  • Optional user accounts
  • Booking system integration
  • Progress indicators

Won't Have

  • Full EMR integration
  • AI medical diagnosis
  • Offline functionality
  • Wearable device integration

Honest Assessment

Gaps & Next Steps

We documented what works, what doesn't, and where to take it next.

Known Gaps

  • Location locked to Halifax — no postal code or geolocation input
  • Directions buttons not functional on pharmacy & hospital tabs
  • Open/closed status and wait time data not live
  • No back-end database of ailments and symptoms
  • No data collection, reporting, or analytics pipeline
  • Consent form exit button unusable
  • No in-app warning that phone numbers will dial

Future Roadmap

  • AI agent for smarter treatment suggestions & symptom identification
  • Power BI dashboard for operational analytics
  • Hospital & pharmacy coverage beyond HRM
  • Google Maps integration for live directions
  • Multi-language support (French, Arabic, Mi'kmaq)
  • User accounts and symptom history tracking
  • Native mobile apps (iOS / Android)
